How to Import Sofas from China to USA: Complete 2026 Guide

If you're a furniture retailer, interior designer, or e-commerce seller in the United States, importing sofas directly from a China sofa factory can significantly boost your profit margins. This guide walks you through every step — from finding the right supplier to receiving goods at your local port.

1. Find a Reliable China Sofa Manufacturer

The first and most critical step is finding a factory that produces quality sofas at competitive prices. Key factors to evaluate:

  • Direct factory vs. trading company: Direct factories offer 20-40% lower prices. Trading companies add markup but may handle logistics better.
  • Product range: A good manufacturer should offer at least 100+ sofa models — fabric recliners, leather sofas, sectional sets, and more.
  • MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity): The best factories accept MOQ as low as 1 piece for trial orders, then scale up for bulk.
  • Customization capability: Can they do custom sizes, colors, fabrics, and OEM branding? This is essential for building your own brand.
  • Export experience: Ensure the factory has experience shipping to USA ports and understands customs documentation.

💡 Pro Tip: Always request factory photos, production line videos, and loading inspection reports before placing your first order. A legitimate factory will provide these without hesitation.

3. Shipping: Sea Freight from China to USA

Sea freight is the most cost-effective shipping method for sofas. There are two main options:

LCL (Less than Container Load)

Best for small orders (1-10 pieces). Your sofas share a container with other shipments.

  • Cost: $60 – $120 per CBM
  • Transit time: 30 – 45 days
  • A typical single recliner is ~0.65 CBM, so shipping cost is roughly $40 – $80 per piece

FCL (Full Container Load)

Best for bulk orders (20+ pieces). You rent an entire container.

  • 20ft container: ~25-28 CBM capacity, cost $2,500 – $4,000
  • 40ft container: ~55-60 CBM capacity, cost $4,500 – $7,000
  • Transit time: 25 – 35 days

4. Customs and Import Duties for USA Sofa Imports

When importing furniture into the USA, you'll need to handle:

  • HTS Code: Most upholstered sofas fall under HTS 9401.61 (upholstered seats with wooden frames)
  • Duty rate: Typically 0% – 5.3% for most sofas from China (check current rates with your customs broker)
  • Documents needed: Commercial Invoice, Bill of Lading, Packing List, Certificate of Origin
  • Customs broker: Hiring a customs broker ($150 – $300 per shipment) is highly recommended for first-time importers

5. Payment Terms: How to Pay Your China Sofa Supplier

Standard payment terms for China furniture factories:

  • 30% T/T deposit to start production
  • 70% T/T balance before shipment (after you receive loading photos and inspection reports)
  • Some suppliers accept Letter of Credit (L/C) for larger orders
  • Never pay 100% upfront. A legitimate factory will work with standard 30/70 terms.

6. Quality Assurance: Protecting Your Investment

Before your sofas ship, ensure quality with these steps:

  1. Request pre-production samples: Get fabric swatches, foam density specs, and mechanism samples
  2. Loading inspection: Ask the factory to send photos of every sofa being loaded into the container
  3. Third-party inspection: For orders over $5,000, consider hiring SGS or Bureau Veritas for quality checks ($200 – $400)
  4. Warranty: A reputable factory should offer at least a 1-year quality warranty

Wholesale Recliner Sofa Buying Guide for USA Retailers

Recliner sofas are one of the best-selling furniture categories in the American market. Whether you run a furniture store, sell on Amazon, or supply interior design projects, understanding the recliner sofa wholesale market is key to maximizing profit. This guide covers everything you need to know.

Why Recliner Sofas Are High-Demand in USA

The USA recliner sofa market is valued at over $8 billion annually and growing. Key demand drivers:

  • Aging population — recliners offer ergonomic comfort
  • Home theater / gaming room trend — reclining sofas are must-haves
  • Work-from-home culture — people invest more in home comfort
  • Price advantage from China — USA retail prices of $800-$2,000 vs. wholesale cost of $150-$400

Material Comparison: Fabric vs. Leather Recliners

Fabric Recliner Sofas

  • Cost: 20-30% cheaper than leather
  • Variety: Hundreds of color and texture options (velvet, linen, chenille, bouclé)
  • Comfort: Generally more breathable and softer
  • Durability: Good quality fabric (Martindale 50,000+) lasts 5-8 years
  • Best for: Family homes, budget-conscious buyers, trendy designs

PU / Faux Leather Recliner Sofas

  • Cost: Mid-range, slightly more than fabric
  • Look: Premium appearance, easy to clean
  • Maintenance: Wipe-clean surface, resistant to stains
  • Durability: Quality PU leather lasts 4-7 years
  • Best for: Offices, modern interiors, easy-maintenance needs

Key Quality Checklist for Wholesale Recliner Orders

Before placing a bulk order, ask your supplier about:

  1. Recliner mechanism: Steel frame mechanism (not plastic) — should withstand 10,000+ cycles
  2. Foam density: Minimum 30D high-density foam for seat cushions
  3. Frame material: Kiln-dried hardwood or solid plywood base — avoid particle board
  4. Fabric grade: Ask for Martindale abrasion test results (minimum 30,000 cycles)
  5. Weight capacity: Standard recliners should support 300+ lbs (136+ kg)
  6. Certifications: Ask about CA117 fire retardancy standard (required for California)

💡 Pro Tip: When ordering wholesale recliners, always order a sample first (1 piece). Test the mechanism, foam quality, and fabric feel before committing to a bulk order. A $150-$200 sample can save you from a $5,000+ mistake.

3. Pros and Cons: China vs Vietnam vs USA

🇨🇳 China — The Overall Winner for Most Importers

  • Pros: Lowest total landed cost, massive supplier base (thousands of factories), best customization options, established logistics infrastructure, fast production (25-35 days), mature quality standards
  • Cons: 5% import duty (vs. 0% for Vietnam), some political/trade tension risk, longer shipping than domestic
  • Best for: Importers who want the lowest cost, widest selection, and full customization capability

🇻🇳 Vietnam — Good Alternative, Limited Scale

  • Pros: Zero duty under USA-Vietnam trade agreements, growing furniture industry, slightly lower labor costs
  • Cons: Fewer sofa factories, less customization experience, longer lead times, less mature logistics, smaller product range
  • Best for: High-volume buyers focused on duty savings, or companies already sourcing other products from Vietnam

🇺🇸 USA Domestic — Premium Segment Only

  • Pros: No shipping delays, no import duties, "Made in USA" marketing appeal, easy quality control visits, fast turnaround
  • Cons: 60-100% higher total cost, limited customization, higher labor costs, fewer manufacturers
  • Best for: Premium/luxury brands, quick-turnaround orders, "Made in USA" marketing positioning

4. The Bottom Line

For the vast majority of USA furniture retailers and wholesalers, China remains the best sourcing option for sofas in 2026. Despite the 5% tariff, China's lower manufacturing costs, massive supplier ecosystem, and superior customization capabilities result in the lowest total landed cost by 30-50% compared to domestic production.

Vietnam is a viable alternative for high-volume importers who can absorb slightly higher logistics costs to save on duties. USA manufacturing makes sense only for premium brands willing to pay a significant premium for domestic production.

💡 Key Takeaway: A 3-seat fabric sofa that costs $800 to make in the USA can be sourced from China for approximately $250-$400 FOB, or $530-$560 landed at a USA port. That's a 35-50% cost advantage for China-sourced sofas.
